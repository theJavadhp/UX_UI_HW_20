Goal

For this project, we were asked to build a modern-day mobile app that helps people plan their next trip for the post-pandemic era.

User Research

Proto Persona

Before conducting the interviews, I created a prototype persona to better understand the target user demographic for the research project. This proto persona served as a foundation for the research and provided a clear representation of the target user, including their age, occupation, goals, pain points, and habits. I used this information to inform the design of the interview questions and guide the overall direction of the research project. By developing a proto persona, I was able to ensure that the research was user-centered and aimed at gathering valuable insights that could be used to inform the design process.

Interviews

In this research project, I was responsible for the planning and execution of 5 interviews with potential users. The objective of these interviews was to gather insights and understand the needs and requirements of the target audience for the product being studied. To achieve this goal, I carefully selected and approached participants who were representative of the target user group. I developed a structured interview guide that covered relevant topics and ensured that all participants were asked consistent questions. During the interviews, I listened attentively to the participants, took thorough notes and recorded their responses. After conducting all 5 interviews, I analyzed the data to extract meaningful insights that informed the design of the application.

Yasman, 22

Masih, 32

Mary, 42

“I usually use the airline website to book the flight because they are more trusted. In fact I prefer to book my flights from an app/website that is more expensive, but has better support”

Interview Data

After conducting interviews with potential users, I used the data collected from the interviews to create a visual representation of the users' thoughts, feelings, and behaviors. To do this, I utilized affinity diagramming to organize and categorize the collected data. Then, using the data from the affinity diagram, I created an empathy map that provided a deeper understanding of the user's perspective by highlighting their pain points, motivations, goals, and habits. Finally, I synthesized the data from the empathy map to create a comprehensive and representative persona that captured the unique characteristics and needs of the potential user. This persona helped me to understand the user's context and design solutions that would meet their specific needs.

Definition & Ideation

Using these findings, I was able to identify a user insight that accurately represented the challenges faced by the users. I then used this insight to develop a problem statement that clearly defined the problem space and provided a direction for the design process. The user insight and problem statement provided a strong foundation for the design phase, ensuring that the solution met the needs of the users and addressed their pain points effectively.

User Insight

“As someone who has limited opportunities to travel throughout the year, Lina needs to be sure about the steps of her trip, because if any part of the trip does not go according to plan, she will be stressed and her trip will be ruined.”

Problem Statement

“Planning a trip can be a complicated and laborious process for many people, as various factors can significantly impact different stages of travel. How might we help users with tight schedules, in creating a reliable itinerary for their trip, ultimately enhancing their travel experience.”
